# Project Brief Framework

These markdown briefs are prep documents for the site content refresh. They are intentionally different from repo `README.md` files.

## Why This Structure

The brief format follows current portfolio and technical case-study guidance:

- Lead with the problem, stakes, and outcome rather than tools.
- Keep the story scannable and business-facing.
- Make decision-making visible.
- Separate architecture and stack from impact so later portfolio pages can compress or expand each section.

## Research Signals Used

- Framer portfolio templates and examples emphasize conversion, trust-building, and clear project framing rather than just visuals.
- Medium-style case study guidance emphasizes a clean arc: problem, data/input, decisions, analysis/system, insights, impact.
- The strongest portfolio case studies show achievements and tradeoffs, not just task lists.

## Brief Template

Each project brief is organized into:

1. Project Thesis
2. Business Problem
3. Outcome and Evidence
4. Key Decision Choices
5. Tech Stack
6. Architecture Snapshot
7. Portfolio Content Angle
8. Evidence Gaps / Refresh Notes

## Intended Use

- Source material for refreshing project pages on the portfolio site
- Source material for blog posts or case-study writeups
- A staging layer between raw repos and final public-facing content
